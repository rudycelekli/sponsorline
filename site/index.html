What verify proves

Promises you can re-run, against a key that can check everything and forge nothing.

Verification runs only against the published public key. The signing secret never leaves your device, so a reviewer can confirm every property below without the ability to fabricate a single entry.

  • Zero code egressNo source, file path, or buffer ever leaves the device. Only allowlisted atoms (lang:* framework:* task:*) are emitted, and verify re-asserts 0 bytes of code across every impression.
  • Consent-gated displayNothing renders until you opt in, within a signed scope and validity window. sponsorline off revokes on the spot.
  • Tamper-evident historyEach impression embeds the prior payload's SHA-256. One Ed25519 signature over the head authenticates the whole ordered chain, so any insert, delete, reorder, or edit breaks verification.
  • Replayable auctionsEvery auction replays deterministically to its recorded outcome. 100% reproducible, machine-independent.
  • No binary patchingIntegration is only through official, documented extension points (Claude Code statusLine, a Cursor adapter). No patching code exists in the tree.
≡ What it does not prove on its own

Verify proves internal consistency under a given key, not whose device produced the log. Publish your public key through a channel a reviewer already trusts (your repo, a signed release) and verify then proves authenticity of origin too. This is the standard public-key trust anchor, stated plainly. We do not ask to be believed.

How it works

A hash chain, a three-prefix allowlist, and a seal.

The mechanism is small enough to read in an afternoon and strict enough that the privacy claim is mechanical, not editorial.

01 / SIGNAL

Coarse interest, on device

Local context is reduced to a versioned, allowlisted vector. Only lang:, framework:, and task: atoms can ever be emitted. A targeting predicate asking for anything else is rejected at ingestion.

02 / AUCTION

Deterministic second-price

A seeded, replayable auction picks the winning sponsor line. Each impression is bound to your signed consent record and appended to a local JSONL witness log, chained by SHA-256.

03 / SEAL

One signature, whole history

A single Ed25519 signature over the chain head vouches for the entire ordered log. Verify costs O(n) cheap hashing plus one signature check, staying well under two seconds even at multi-year volume.

Guardrails, enforced

We made the bad outcomes impossible, then wrote the test that proves it.

The two failure modes that would quietly destroy this product are not policy promises. They are invariants the build refuses to violate.

Static egress guard

The moat cannot be edited away

Routing spend through an ad-provider structurally requires importing their identity graph. A source scan fails the build if any file references an ad, tracking, or analytics domain, or a cross-site identity token. The only external host allowed is the payout rail.

k-anon supply floor

Thin reach is withheld, not sold

A campaign that reaches too few distinct devices to be anonymous or meaningful is never presented as a payable number. The aggregate withholds anything under a 200-device floor and surfaces it as suppressed instead.
