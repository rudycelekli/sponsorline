Premortem · Prospective Hindsight

Founding-Sponsor Seed Plan

Sponsorline — bootstrapping the two-sided network from zero without a marketer portal, billing custody, or ad-provider integration.
Frame: it is ~6 months out; the plan failed Method: Gary Klein, 8 parallel investigators Date: 2026-06-14

Synthesis — read this first

Most likely failure

The two-sided cold-start deadlock in concierge form (01 + 03 + 04). The load-bearing premise — “keep the supply-on to demand-on gap to days” — is false: serial two-sided concierge sales by one founder takes weeks-to-months, and in that gap the honest, stranger-verifiable receipt chain turns the emptiness into a credible, quotable public artifact that burns the exact evangelist cohort you need.

Most dangerous failure

Flat-fee seed economics making verify attest to fiction (05), tied with a Stripe balance freeze (08). 05 is irreversible and strikes the core: verify/reconcile() today prove internal consistency, not real-money provenance. Once “verifiable” is shown to be theater on the most scrutiny-prone cohort, the moat is gone for good.

The hidden assumption

That you can seed the network with shortcuts (synthetic auction bids, flat off-platform fees, goodwill sponsors, manual everything) without those shortcuts dissolving the very properties the seed exists to prove. The sharpest form: “verify” currently proves the math is consistent with itself, not that the money is real — and the seed is exactly where that bites.

Failure deep-dives

FAILURE 01High

Sponsors won’t pre-fund; reach can’t accrue first

At recruit time the segment has ~0 devices, so aggregateReceipts returns suppressed/empty by design. Every buyer sees a literal zero on the only number that matters and says “come back when you have the audience” — but the audience is what the money was meant to seed. The receipt chain’s refusal to lie about reach is what stops either side going first.
Assumption
Founding sponsors will pay on the promise of verifiable reach rather than demanding proven reach first.
Warning signs
First calls end with “send audience numbers first”; suppressedCampaigns > 0 and reachDevices = 0 for weeks while opt-ins stay flat.
FAILURE 02High

The k-anon-clearing segment isn’t a buyable segment

We picked lang:python because it was the only thing that could clear 200 in weeks. We hit 240 — then every sponsor asked “who are these people?” and the only honest answer was “people who write Python.” Narrowing to framework:django + task:database dropped to ~30 and the floor refused to emit. We could prove a segment nobody buys, or describe one we could never prove.
Assumption
A privacy-provable broad segment has buyer value, when spend pays for the exact qualification the 3-prefix vocabulary + k-anon floor forbid.
Warning signs
First sponsor question is always “can you narrow to X?”; every narrowing drops below 200 or isn’t expressible; sponsors only agree to an “experiment” flat fee with no renewal line.
FAILURE 03High

Developer churn in the supply-on → demand-on gap

~300 opt-ins in three weeks; closing one sponsor took six-to-eight weeks, not days. Devs saw the plain “Thinking…” line earning nothing. Because earnings are signed and stranger-verifiable, “$0.03 over two weeks, toggled off” became a credible, screenshotable artifact. The trust moat made the cold-start emptiness quotable, and evangelists opt in only once.
Assumption
The gap is a scheduling problem (days), not a structural consequence of serial two-sided concierge sales (weeks-to-months).
Warning signs
Median days-since-opt-in with zero paid receipts climbing past ~7; first public signed-receipt screenshot of $0 earnings on HN/Discord.
FAILURE 04High

Concierge doesn’t scale; founder bottleneck + reconciliation drift

Sponsors 1-5 felt like proof at ~3 founder-hours each. Then the same person had to ship npm patches, fix the status bar, recruit 100 more devs AND onboard sponsors 6-10. The quiet killer: by-hand payout matching against signed receipts is exact work done in stolen minutes. A flat fee got entered as a daily cap; payout cycles slipped. The k-anon floor held; the operator didn’t.
Assumption
Concierge effort scales sub-linearly, when it’s fixed-cost-per-unit work competing for the same person’s hours as engineering and supply.
Warning signs
Onboarding lead time creeping past a week, or a queue of “verbally committed, not loaded”; first reconciliation mismatch, especially one caught by a sponsor or dev.
FAILURE 05Critical

Flat-fee economics make verify truthful about fiction

The auction needs bids; a flat-fee sponsor never submitted one, so the operator seeds a synthetic bidCents. Each impression records a clearingPriceCents, the ledger accrues floor(clearing/2), verify exits 0 — internally consistent and entirely circular. reconcile() only proves developerBalance == sum(clearingPrices)/2, a tautology over operator-chosen numbers. The seed shortcut didn’t break verify; it made verify truthful about fiction. Once exposed as theater, trust doesn’t come back.
Assumption
Cryptographically replaying the auction proves earnings are real, when it only proves the recorded numbers agree with each other.
Warning signs
clearingPriceCents traces to operator-set synthetic bids; flat_fee / impressions reconciles to no receipt; verify green while money stops at DryRunGateway.
FAILURE 06High

No attribution → goodwill spend → no renewal

Eight sponsors signed on the founder’s network and a clean values pitch. The flight delivered exactly what was promised. Then Q3 planning hit: each lead defended line items against a CAC/ROAS spreadsheet, and Sponsorline’s row had one number (impressions) and a blank where pipeline belongs. The champion couldn’t translate goodwill into a renewal the growth team would approve. Five of eight churned silently; zero renewed; the “≥2 renew” gate failed.
Assumption
Sponsors who buy on values will renew on values, when they buy on values once but renew only against a number finance accepts.
Warning signs
Sponsors ask “how will we know it worked?” and accept a vague answer; the signer is a founder/VP, not the growth owner who controls recurring budget.
FAILURE 07Medium

Inconsistent manual creative review detonates the brand

Sponsor #6 asked to swap in a “punchier” line: “Ship 10x faster, your competitors already are.” With no codified rubric, the operator read it as borderline-acceptable and loaded it. A dev screenshotted it beneath Sponsorline’s own copy (“addictive products earn attention, they don’t demand it”) with “demand it, apparently.” The thread’s point: judgment-based review is only as strong as the most rushed operator on the worst day.
Assumption
An operator’s in-the-moment taste is a reliable, repeatable substitute for a written, enforceable creative standard.
Warning signs
Two verdicts on a near-identical line with no written tie-breaker; any approved line uses comparative framing, urgency, or imperative CTA and gets loaded anyway.
FAILURE 08Critical

Fee pool re-creates the custody / money-transmitter shape

Off-platform flat fees landed in Sponsorline’s Stripe balance: one commingled pool, sponsor dollars in, fanned out to ~300 unrelated devs via /v1/transfers from the platform’s own key. By the third batch, Stripe’s review saw inbound fees and immediate fan-out to hundreds of new Express accounts with no underlying marketplace transaction, and placed a reserve/hold. “Dollars paid and reconciled” inverted to “receipts exist, dollars frozen.” Devs crossing $600 had no W-9/1099 path.
Assumption
Pricing a fee “flat” and invoicing “off-platform” changes the regulatory shape, when only the flow (collect, hold, select, fan-out) determines it — and the flow was unchanged.
Warning signs
Fee money sits in Sponsorline’s balance before any dev is paid; cumulative US payout approaches $600 with no W-9 on file and no 1099 path in code.

Revised plan

  1. Anchor money to receipts before seeding (fixes 05, 08). Either have founding sponsors submit real per-impression bids through the MCP handler, or explicitly label the seed a fixed-reward pilot and stop marketing verify as proof of market earnings. Add a signed settlement record linking each real payment to its impression batch so reconcile() ties to real dollars. Publicly separate the two claims: verify proves privacy + chain integrity (true now) vs proves you were paid a real market price (not true until money is anchored).
  2. Do not custody (fixes 08). Use Stripe destination charges / on_behalf_of so sponsor money never lands in Sponsorline’s balance; decide W-9/1099 before any US dev nears $600, or cap seed payouts well under the threshold with a documented stipend posture.
  3. Choose the segment by buyer-demand AND k-anon jointly (fixes 02). Before recruiting devs, confirm with 3-5 sponsors that a 200+-clearable 3-prefix segment is worth paying for. If none is, that’s a vocabulary / business-model decision to make before seeding (accept CPM brand-sponsorship framing rather than performance targeting).
  4. Pre-sell demand to break the deadlock (fixes 01, 03). Get written contingent commitments (“we fund $X when you reach 200 devices in segment Y”) from 3-5 sponsors before recruiting devs. Sequence: soft-circle demand → recruit supply to the floor → flip demand live within days.
  5. Codify the creative standard (fixes 07). One-page written policy (no comparative framing, no urgency/FOMO, no imperative-shaming CTA, mandatory clear label), two-person sign-off, logged approval enforced at submit time, not operator vibe.
  6. Sell on a renewable, privacy-safe metric, not goodwill (fixes 06). Define what a sponsor can renew against without attribution (DP-aggregate reach + frequency, plus an optional consented on-device aggregate-only signal above k-anon). Sell to the growth owner; set the renewal metric at sale time. If no privacy-safe metric exists, sell these honestly as brand sponsorships.
  7. Tool the concierge without building a portal (fixes 04). Script campaign loading and a reconciliation check that matches off-platform payments to signed-receipt batches and flags drift; template onboarding. Internal tooling, not a marketer-facing product.

Pre-launch checklist

  • 3-5 sponsors give written contingent commitments for a specific 200+-clearable 3-prefix segment BEFORE dev recruiting begins.
  • Real-money anchor in place: a signed settlement record links each sponsor payment to its impression batch and reconcile() ties to real dollars — OR the seed is explicitly a fixed-reward pilot and no marketing claims verify proves market earnings.
  • No sponsor funds land in Sponsorline’s Stripe balance (destination charges / on_behalf_of), OR seed payouts capped under tax thresholds with a documented stipend posture; W-9/1099 path decided.
  • Written creative policy + two-person sign-off + logged approvals, enforced at submit time.
  • Renewal metric agreed with each sponsor’s growth owner at sale time (privacy-safe), not goodwill.
  • Reconciliation script flags payment-vs-receipt drift automatically.
  • Median “days opted-in with zero paid receipts” monitored; supply recruiting paced to the demand-live date.
