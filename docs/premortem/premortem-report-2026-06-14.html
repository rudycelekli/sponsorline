Premortem — Marketer Portal + Ad-Provider Integration

Sponsorline · the AI-dev ad network that never sees your code

9 investigators ran in parallel Method: Gary Klein prospective hindsight Frame: it is late 2026, this already failed
What was premortemed: Build a marketer self-serve portal (sign up → fund a balance → build a campaign → set bid/budget/targeting → launch → read reach reports) plus integrations into existing ad providers (Google Ads, Meta) to route/aggregate ad spend into Sponsorline. Success = marketers self-serve onboard, fund, and run campaigns; spend flows in; the network grows on both sides without breaking the "never sees your code" promise.

Synthesis — read this first

Most likely failure

You build the demand side onto a supply side that doesn't exist yet.

Two independent investigators converged here from different angles (empty-inventory cold start; wrong-side-first). The killer detail is in your own code: the DP engine refuses to emit an aggregate below roughly 200 contributing devices, and a deterministic auction against a few hundred thin, single-language installs returns near-empty winner sets. A funded marketer hits Launch, the most polished screen in the product renders a flat line, and the honest signed-receipt count becomes the murder weapon — you can't fudge it. Self-serve makes the emptiness public in a way a concierge sales motion never would.

Most dangerous failure

The ad-provider integration silently detonates the moat.

This is the one to insure against because it is irreversible. Making spend "fungible" with Google/Meta structurally requires speaking their identity-and-attribution graph: importing Customer Match / Custom Audience segments (hashed-email-derived IDs) and firing conversion pings back (gclid/fbclid). That widens the one-line trust boundary — your 3-entry ALLOWLIST.prefixes array — and opens an outbound channel carrying exactly the PII-adjacent data your homepage swears does not exist. An enterprise reviewer doing what your pitch invites (read the code, don't trust the vendor) diffs targeting.ts, screenshots it next to the marketing copy, and the brand is the contradiction. Tied for danger: a Stripe balance freeze if you custody advertiser funds (money-transmitter shape).

The hidden assumption

That you can bolt a conventional ad-network demand side onto Sponsorline without it becoming a conventional ad network.

Every one of the nine stories traces back to this. "Demand will pull supply into existence." "Money-in is the mirror of money-out." "Verifiability is a dev-side feature." "Spend can be made interoperable with Google/Meta." "Stripe handles compliance." Each is the same root assumption wearing a different hat: that the parts of an ordinary ad business (a hosted portal, provider integrations, held balances) can be added without dissolving the single property — trustless verifiability with zero identity data — that is the entire reason Sponsorline is worth building.

The revised plan

  1. Sequence supply-first. Do not build the portal yet. Next phase = developer opt-in: the install + consent moment, the "why turn this on" value, crossing the ~200-device DP floor per targetable segment. Hard gate: no marketer UI until real reach reports can emit on real devices.
  2. Run demand concierge, not self-serve. The MCP marketer handlers already exist (marketer_submit_campaign, marketer_report). Onboard the first 3–5 advertisers by hand and invoice manually. Zero portal code until there's proven pull.
  3. Kill "siphon via Google/Meta APIs" as designed. It violates their ToS and requires their identity graph. Replace with a one-way, manual import of creative + budget intent only (paste/CSV — no OAuth, no audience import, no attribution callback). Turn it into a feature: "bring your creative; we never touch your Google data."
  4. Make the trust boundary a tripwire. Any PR touching ALLOWLIST.prefixes or adding an outbound call to a *.facebook.com/googleadservices.com endpoint fails CI by default and needs explicit sign-off. Add a test that forbids new prefixes.
  5. Decide fund-custody posture before any money-in. Strongly prefer not holding balances — route via Stripe so Sponsorline is never the custodian (destination charges / on_behalf_of), sidestepping money-transmitter exposure. Wire W-9 collection + 1099 generation before flipping SPONSORLINE_LIVE_PAYOUTS.
  6. Build the charge/escrow/meter primitive deliberately, separate from any UI. The real 80% is money-in: Ledger.settle()/payout() must stop being never-returning stubs and reconcile against asynchronous signed receipts before a portal exists.
  7. Adversarial fraud test with real-money incentives before launch. Simulate low-volume sybil farms (the deliberately-deferred T4 gap) and self-dealing rings; build fund-to-payout correlation detection so the 96/day ceiling isn't your only — and disputable — defense.

Pre-launch checklist

  • ☐ Opted-in active device count per targetable segment ≥ ~200 (DP k-anon floor); a real reach query emits, not "below threshold."
  • ☐ Zero new allowlist prefixes; zero outbound calls to ad-provider endpoints carrying receipts/IDs (grep + CI test green).
  • ☐ Fund-custody / merchant-of-record posture reviewed by payments counsel; W-9/1099 pipeline live before any real payout.
  • ☐ Ledger.settle()/payout() implemented with receipt reconciliation — no stubs anywhere in the money path.
  • ☐ Adversarial sybil + self-dealing simulation run; fund-to-payout correlation detection in place.

The nine failure modes

Likelihood: High
Failure 01

Empty-inventory cold start

Marketer funds, builds a lang:python / framework:react / task:refactor campaign, hits Launch — the auction matches ~40 mostly-idle devices and the reach screen renders a flat line. Refund on day three; "vaporware" hits a marketing Slack and a podcast. The honest signed receipt count is the murder weapon: too truthful to fudge.

Underlying assumption

That developer supply would exist (or self-arrive) by the time marketers showed up.

Early warning

Targetable-segment device counts in the dozens, yet the portal allows launch with no reach floor; first campaigns show available-reach far below requested.

Severity: Critical
Failure 02

Billing-in is the real product and it's unbuilt

The slick builder gets the GA date pulled forward; nobody points at ledger.ts, where settle()/payout() are never-returning "v0.3" stubs and there's no advertiser-funded balance to debit. The "almost done" billing plumbing drags four months; real self-serve money never flows. Design partners get invoiced by hand in a spreadsheet.

Underlying assumption

That taking money in is the mirror image of paying money out, so payout discipline carries over.

Early warning

The only working real-money path is money out (StripeConnectGateway.pay); no charge/authorization/escrow primitive exists; advertisers invoiced manually.

Severity: Brand-fatal
Failure 03

Ad integration breaks the privacy promise

"Import my audiences" adds an audience: allowlist prefix mapping hashed-email Customer Match / Custom Audience IDs; an attribution callback fires gclid/fbclid back to Google/Meta. The 3-line trust boundary is widened to wave PII through. A reviewer diffs targeting.ts, screenshots it beside "no such data exists." The contradiction is the product.

Underlying assumption

That spend can be made interoperable with Google/Meta without importing their identity-and-attribution substrate.

Early warning

Any PR adding to ALLOWLIST.prefixes; any outbound call to a Google/Meta endpoint outside the C2 DP-egress gate, carrying a receipt or click ID.

Severity: High
Failure 04

Hosted portal contradicts "no vendor trust"

To fill marketer dashboards, the server becomes source of truth for balances and spend. A championing dev asks on HN: "if the auction and my 50% split are computed server-side from a DB I can't clone, what does verify verify?" The honest answer requires the words "you trust the server." The wedge that made you not-a-commodity is gone.

Underlying assumption

That verifiability is a dev-side feature you can keep on one side, not the property that makes the whole market trustless.

Early warning

A design doc declares "server is source of truth for balances/spend"; a developer publicly asks what verify still proves.

Severity: Critical
Failure 05

Fraud integrity collapses with real money

The 96/day ceiling becomes the farming target, not the deterrent: keys minting 40–80 plausible impressions/day stay in clean[] forever. Self-dealing rings (advertiser funds, colluding devs collect the 50% split) launder spend through Stripe — nothing forged, all caps honored. Marketers with lawyers demand an audit you can't win: provably sealed, not provably human or real.

Underlying assumption

That cryptographic authenticity + a physical ceiling is sufficient — when the system never proved economic legitimacy.

Early warning

Rising share of clean[] receipts clustered just under 96; new pubkeys outpacing plausible install growth; fund and payout sides resolving to overlapping identity/IP/timing clusters.

Likelihood: High
Failure 06

Built the wrong side first

Demand is the easy side to build against fixtures, so it raced ahead — full monetization spine signing receipts against a thin sliver of pilot devices. First real marketer asks: "how many opted-in devs, and can you prove they're not synthetic?" The answer is below the DP k-anon floor. The differentiated asset exists but can't be reported on without breaking the invariant that is the brand.

Underlying assumption

That demand would pull supply into existence, rather than supply being the precondition the portal can't function without.

Early warning

Marketer-backend commit velocity rising while active device count stays under ~200; first reach query returns "below k-anonymity threshold, cannot emit."

Severity: High
Failure 07

Ad-provider ToS / dependency risk

"Route your Google budget into Sponsorline" becomes the top channel — via OAuth into Google Ads/Meta, reading campaign and audience data to auto-generate equivalents. Both platforms' terms prohibit using API data to build a competing product or export audiences. A compliance review (triggered by your own landing-page copy) issues a 30-day notice; you can't remediate because the feature is the violation. Keys suspended, channel dead overnight.

Underlying assumption

That platform API access is a neutral utility, not a permission the incumbent competitor revokes the moment you use it to take their revenue.

Early warning

The feature's core action is named in the providers' prohibited-uses clauses; one integration becomes >40% of signups while OAuth scopes keep expanding and reviews sit longer each cycle.

Severity: High
Failure 08

Scope explosion sinks the launch

"One push" becomes five products at once — auth, payments, campaign-builder, bidding, provider adapters. Integrations stall on others' OAuth/review queues; the bid loop collides with the deterministic auction/prng invariants. The 178-test discipline that was the edge slips to "backfill later." The polished dev-side network can't ship because it's coupled to half-built demand parts.

Underlying assumption

That a two-sided market must launch with both sides complete — so all five demand products precede shipping the finished supply side.

Early warning

Test-to-surface ratio inverts (new packages land sparse/skipped); standups fill with external blockers ("waiting on Meta approval") instead of owned progress.

Severity: Company-fatal
Failure 09

Money-transmitter / regulatory exposure

Eleven good days, then Stripe's risk engine sees the textbook unlicensed-money-transmission shape: customer money into your balance, held for days, fanned out to unrelated third parties across borders. The balance freezes — every funded-but-undisbursed dollar locked. Plus ~140 US devs paid, several over $600, zero W-9s, no 1099 pipeline (explicitly out-of-scope, never enabled). Six-week freeze, chargebacks, dead.

Underlying assumption

That Stripe Connect's payout-side mitigations also absolve you of custodying advertiser funds and being de facto payor.

Early warning

Stripe "verify your business model" emails / a rolling reserve appearing; first US dev crosses $600 cumulative with no W-9 on file and no 1099 line generated.
