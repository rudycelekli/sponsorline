Premortem · prospective hindsight

The Sponsorline public launch has failed

It is December 2026. The launch is done and it did not work. Seven investigators each took one way it could have died and wrote the case study. Below is the synthesis, then every failure mode in full.

What: public launch of a consent-first, zero-egress AI-dev sponsor line Who: developers (supply), enterprises, marketers (demand) Win: verify claim survives scrutiny · devs opt in · no trust/legal blowup Investigators: 7 (parallel)

Synthesis — read this first

Most likely failure
HIGH PROBABILITY
Two-sided cold start: "verify once, then off." Developers run npx sponsorline verify, say "neat," and turn it off within a week because earnings are structurally $0 until a single segment crosses the ~200-device k-anon floor. Density never forms, no segment is sellable, no advertiser, no revenue, no reason to keep it on. The privacy floor that protects users also makes the cold start nearly impossible. This needs no villain, just ordinary behavior, which is why it is the most probable.
Most dangerous failure
CONFIRMED LIVE RISK
A trust failure on a trust-only product. The exposed npm token npm_VHb7…uEPow is still live, in plaintext, in ~/Downloads/claudeAds/Untitled (verified during this premortem; untracked by git but on disk). If it is published-with by accident or reused before revocation, an attacker ships a malicious sponsorline version to the exact security-conscious audience the product courts. For a "don't trust us, verify" brand this is permanent, not a bad week. Insure against it before anything else ships.
The hidden assumption
THE REAL LESSON
Across every failure, the project quietly substitutes "we built / we asserted X" for "X is independently verified or permitted." Documented extension point = permitted ad surface (it isn't, no platform sign-off on file). A signed, consistent ledger = proof of zero egress (it isn't, it proves the diary, not the wire). "I'll revoke the token" = it is revoked (it isn't). "Earn 50%" = devs can be paid (they can't, no live rail). Ironic for a product whose whole pitch is don't trust, verify. Apply that pitch to yourself.
The revised plan
  1. Verify the token is dead, don't assert it. Revoke at npmjs.com, confirm with npm token list, delete Untitled, enable npm 2FA + provenance, publish from a clean env. (→ most dangerous)
  2. Get platform permission in writing, or disclose the risk. Confirm Anthropic + Cursor allow third-party sponsor content via status line, or reframe as "your status line, you choose to monetize it" and state the platform risk openly. Do not stake the launch on an unsanctioned surface. (→ host-tool risk)
  3. Stop claiming verify proves zero-egress. It proves consent + auction-history integrity. Back the egress claim with the auditable open-source client, a reproducible build, and an open invitation to tcpdump/strace it. Kill "verify both claims in one command." (→ verify gap, cheapest highest-leverage fix)
  4. Reframe the headline to defuse "ads in my editor." Lead with reclaiming dead status-line space on the developer's terms with provable cleanliness, not "I turned on an ad network." Pre-seed 3-5 trusted devs who engage on mechanism. (→ backlash)
  5. Don't promise money you can't pay yet. Reword "Developers earn 50%" to a conditional, forward-looking line until the live Stripe rail + KYC + thresholds exist. Show a transparent live "devices per segment" counter; earnings arrive after density. (→ cold start + payout reality)
  6. Protect the one-person launch window. Freeze and smoke-test the npm funnel from a clean machine hours before announce, not at announce. Pre-write the top-5 objection replies. No concierge sales in the first 48h. (→ bandwidth)
Pre-launch checklist
  • npm token list shows the exposed token gone · Untitled deleted · 2FA on · publishing from clean env
  • Written platform answer (or public risk disclosure) on status-line sponsor content for both Claude Code and Cursor
  • Launch copy no longer says verify proves zero-egress; egress backed by auditable client + reproducible build + audit invitation
  • "Earn 50%" reworded to conditional; no unpayable balances accrue
  • npm funnel smoke-tested from a fresh machine ≥3h before announce; top-5 HN objection replies pre-written

The seven failure modes

One investigator per mode, run in parallel. Each returned a failure story, the underlying assumption, and early warning signs.

Failure 01
STRUCTURAL

Host-tool platform risk

Status line is host-governed chrome (Claude Code's statusLine command, Cursor's createStatusBarItem), built for the developer's own context, not third-party ads. A single upstream changelog line (sandbox output, rate-limit redraws, "no commercial content") silently zeroes the inventory. Enterprises' first call is to the vendor, who confirms it's unsanctioned, so the clean-egress proof never gets read.

Underlying assumption
A documented, unpatched extension point is also a contractually and durably permitted one for third-party monetization.
Early warning signs
  • No written platform authorization on file, only extension-point mechanics.
  • Redraw cadence, line length, escape handling are all host-decided; motion already ships off-by-default.
Failure 02
NARRATIVE-FATAL

verify proves the wrong thing

cmd-verify.ts reads only the local, client-authored ledger: it checks consent signatures and replays the witness log. That proves internal consistency, not what the process put on the wire. The security crowd's verdict: "you're verifying your own diary." The private backend can't be audited at all. The one-command demo meant to win HN becomes the artifact used to discredit it.

Underlying assumption
Cryptographic consistency of a signed ledger is interchangeable with a guarantee about what the client actually transmitted.
Early warning signs
  • SECURITY.md attributes zero-egress to a source-property while selling verify as the proof.
  • verify has no network capture or egress-path analysis anywhere in its path.
Failure 03
MOST LIKELY

Two-sided cold start

Verify is a one-time party trick; the status line is a daily tax. Installs smear thinly across lang:* / framework:* cells; no single cell holds 200 simultaneously-active devices, churn empties the ones that fill. Earnings are $0 by construction, so the only rational reason to keep it on is unavailable until density exists, and density needs people to keep it on. The flywheel never turns.

Underlying assumption
Developers will keep an in-editor ad on for the promise of earnings that the k-anon floor guarantees are exactly $0 until a density no organic launch reaches.
Early warning signs
  • High week-1 init/verify, day-7 retention in low single digits.
  • Max active devices per segment plateaus far below 200 even as installs climb.
Failure 04
REPUTATIONAL

"Ads in my editor" backlash

LinkedIn rewards confidence; HN holds a referendum on the category, not the code. The headline "an ad network inside my coding tool" does the framing, and the framing is a confession. "Earn 50%" reads as selling your attention back for pennies; "inevitable trend" is clipped as the smoking gun. Because brand and founder name are the same surface, the dunk is personal, durable, and searchable.

Underlying assumption
Developers judge the product on how cleanly the ads are implemented, not on the fact that there are ads at all.
Early warning signs
  • One-sentence pitch makes a dev friend wince or joke, not ask how it works.
  • Top comments engage the category ("ads in my editor"), not the mechanism (does it really not see my code).
Failure 05
MOST DANGEROUS

Trust failure on a trust product

Confirmed: the live npm token sits in plaintext in an un-gitignored scratch file (Untitled), never proven revoked. "Step 0: revoke" was treated as a checkbox, not a gate. A hijacked sponsorline@0.3.1 with a postinstall script now does see developer code, hitting the exact security-conscious audience. Ed25519 doesn't save it: npm publish trust is the token, not the seal, and the two chains were never connected.

Underlying assumption
Security hygiene is a task to complete ("I'll revoke it") rather than a gate to prove ("it is revoked and no copy exists on disk").
Early warning signs
  • A live secret in plaintext, outside the repo and outside .gitignore (verified present).
  • Runbook never proves the token dead (npm token list) before publish, revocation is asserted not verified.
Failure 06
EXECUTION

Solo-founder bandwidth collapse

One launch window where attention, scrutiny, and curiosity peak at once, and one person can occupy only one room. init breaks on a registry-cache propagation issue while the top HN comment questions zero-egress. Fixing the funnel and answering the crowd are mutually exclusive in real time. The unanswered comment hardens into the accepted read; the demand/supply motion sits untouched. By December the product is inert, not dead.

Underlying assumption
Launch-day defense, bug-fixing, and the demand/supply motion arrive sequentially, so one person can time-slice across them.
Early warning signs
  • Runbook assigns one person to "be present" while the steps most likely to break happen at announce time, no backup named.
  • A hostile top HN comment on the core claim sits unanswered >30 min during peak traffic.
Failure 07
PROMISE GAP

"Earn 50%" outruns payout reality

The only shipped gateway is DryRunGateway (live=false); the live Stripe Connect rail is deliberately unbuilt. Balances accrue but can't leave. The first real payout interaction is a government-ID KYC flow for $3, below the $10 minPayoutCents floor. Devs post "I have to do KYC to withdraw $3 I can't even reach the minimum for." Or, to ship fast, funds get pooled and fanned out, the money-transmitter shape Stripe freezes.

Underlying assumption
Proving a developer is owed money is the hard part, when the hard part is the unbuilt legal/operational rail that actually pays them.
Early warning signs
  • Repo ships only DryRunGateway while marketing commits to "earn 50%."
  • Settlement log paidCents stays 0 while balances climb; median balance below the $10 floor.
